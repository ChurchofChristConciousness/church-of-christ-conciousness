The Christ Consciousness Church is governed through an ecclesiastical trust structure established for the sole purpose of spiritual stewardship, protection of sacred property, and service to the public good.

This trust exists independently of commercial enterprise and political activity, ensuring that all assets, records, and decisions remain aligned with religious purpose and lawful governance.

Trustees act as fiduciaries, bound by conscience, duty, and law to preserve the integrity of the Church’s mission, property, and doctrine.

Governance is conducted transparently, in harmony with divine principles, ecclesiastical tradition, and applicable civil law, without surrender of religious autonomy.
